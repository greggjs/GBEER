Gene Block Evolution, Evaluation, and Research


Gene blocks are proximal genes in the bacterial genome that are suspected of operating together. Known special cases of gene blocks are operons, where the genes are co-transcribed in a polycistronic mRNA. Several models have been proposed to explain observed patterns of gene block evolution. However, there is no universal method to describe gene block evolution, which is necessary for a comprehensive examination of the forces affecting this genomic feature. GBEER offers researchers a graphical method to examine how gene blocks evolve on a case-by-case basis.
